Title: Cognitive Reframing Steps
Category: Cognitive Restructuring

Overview:
Cognitive reframing is the process of identifying negative interpretations and replacing them with balanced perspectives.

Steps:
1. Identify the distressing thought.
2. Evaluate factual accuracy.
3. Consider alternative explanations.
4. Ask what you would say to a friend.
5. Formulate a balanced conclusion.

Why It Works:
Thoughts influence emotions. Adjusting interpretation changes emotional response.

Therapeutic Goal:
To reduce anxiety and depressive thinking patterns.
